##  Project Overview

Built as a structured learning project, this Power BI dashboard consolidates 
Blinkit's grocery retail data — spanning product types, outlet formats, fat content, 
location tiers, and establishment history — into one filterable intelligence view 
for sales, inventory, and customer strategy teams.

| Detail | Info |
|--------|------|
| 🛠️ Tools | Power BI, Power Query, DAX |
| 📊 Dataset | 8,000+ grocery retail records |
| 🏢 Domain | Retail & Quick Commerce |
| 📅 Type | Structured Learning Project |

---

##  Problem Statement

Blinkit's retail data across product categories, outlet formats, and geographies 
lacked a unified view. Without centralized visibility, decisions around inventory 
planning, outlet benchmarking, and customer engagement had no single source of truth.

---

## Approach

- Cleaned 8,000+ records, resolving inconsistencies across product 
  attributes and outlet formats
- Created DAX measures for Total Sales, Average Sales, Item Count, 
  and Average Rating
- Built a **dynamic METRICS slicer** toggling all visuals between 
  4 KPIs simultaneously — usable by both ops and strategy teams
- Designed KPI cards, line chart, donut charts, clustered bar, 
  funnel chart, and pivot table — each answering a specific business question
- Mapped fat content, outlet size, location tier, and establishment 
  year against sales performance

## What the Data Revealed

### Newer Outlets Outperform Older Ones
> Total Sales grew **68%** from ₹78K (2011) to ₹131K (2022) — 
> reflecting improved expansion strategy and operational maturity over time

### Low Fat Demand is Strongest in Tier 3, Not Tier 1
> Tier 3 generates **₹307K** in Low Fat sales vs **₹215K** in Tier 1 — 
> a **43% gap** that challenges the assumption that health-conscious 
> buying is a premium-tier behaviour

### Format Doesn't Drive Transaction Value
> Supermarket Type 1 and Grocery Stores show near-identical avg sales 
> (**₹141 vs ₹140**) — outlet format alone isn't a differentiator 
> in per-transaction performance

### Grocery Stores Carry 3x More Item Visibility
> Visibility scores of **0.10 vs 0.03** (Supermarket Type 2) — 
> Grocery stores surface a far broader catalogue, yet avg sales 
> remain equal — raising a key question about visibility-to-revenue conversion
